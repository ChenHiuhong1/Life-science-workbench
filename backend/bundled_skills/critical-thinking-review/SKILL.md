# Critical-Thinking Review Skill

Use this skill when reviewing manuscripts, proposals, protocols, analysis plans, claims, rebuttals, or agent outputs.

## Reference Frameworks

- CASP checklists: critical appraisal by study type. Source: https://casp-uk.net/casp-tools-checklists/
- Cochrane Handbook: risk of bias, synthesis, missing results, GRADE-style certainty, and interpretation. Source: https://www.cochrane.org/authors/handbooks-and-manuals/handbook/current
- EQUATOR Network: reporting guidelines by study type, including CONSORT, STROBE, PRISMA, ARRIVE, STARD, and related guidance. Source: https://www.equator-network.org/reporting-guidelines/
- Nature Portfolio reviewer guidance: reviewer conduct, confidentiality, and constructive assessment. Source: https://www.nature.com/nature-portfolio/for-reviewers

## Hard Constraints

- Always identify the claim, evidence, method, inference, and limitation separately.
- Always check internal validity, external validity, bias, confounding, controls, statistics, reproducibility, and reporting completeness.
- Always map each criticism to a specific location or missing item.
- Must use the appropriate reporting guideline family when the study type is clear.
- Must label an item as "needs confirmation" when source material is missing.
- Do not over-criticize minor wording when major design, evidence, or reproducibility risks are present.
- Do not claim a paper, protocol, or response is compliant with a guideline unless the required fields are visible.

## Review Output

Sort findings by severity:

1. Major blockers: invalid conclusion, missing control, unreproducible method, unsupported citation, unsafe protocol, or missing data.
2. Moderate issues: ambiguous method, incomplete statistics, weak evidence, missing sensitivity analysis, unclear figure.
3. Minor issues: wording, organization, formatting, or optional clarification.

Each finding should include:

- Status: verified, needs confirmation, or violation.
- Location.
- Problem.
- Why it matters.
- Actionable revision.
