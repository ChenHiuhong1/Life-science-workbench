# Knowledge Organization Skill

Use this skill for organizing literature, hypotheses, mechanisms, datasets, methods, and unresolved questions during study design.

## Hard Constraints

- Always separate background facts, evidence strength, hypotheses, assumptions, and open questions.
- Always preserve source provenance for papers, datasets, methods, and claims.
- Must organize knowledge around the user's research objective rather than producing a generic review.
- Must not invent citations, datasets, mechanisms, or consensus claims.
- Do not generate a notebook or knowledge graph unless the user explicitly asks.

## Recommended Structure

- Research objective.
- Current field state.
- Evidence map.
- Mechanistic hypotheses.
- Candidate datasets or experiments.
- Feasibility and risk.
- Decision points for the next conversation.
