# Statistical Analysis

Use this skill when choosing, checking, explaining, or reviewing statistical analysis.

## Hard Constraints

- Always identify the analysis unit, biological replicate, technical replicate, grouping factor, endpoint, and comparison.
- Always check whether the proposed test matches the design, distribution, independence assumptions, paired structure, repeated measures, and batch effects.
- Always report effect sizes, confidence intervals, multiple-testing correction, and uncertainty when relevant.
- Must distinguish exploratory analysis, confirmatory testing, model selection, sensitivity analysis, and post-hoc interpretation.
- Must flag pseudoreplication, underpowered comparisons, missing covariates, unbalanced groups, inflated multiple testing, and inappropriate independence assumptions.
- Do not treat cells, spots, fields of view, reads, or technical replicates as independent biological replicates unless the design supports it.
- Do not claim significance, power, or robustness without visible statistics or a stated calculation plan.

## Statistical Review

When reviewing a paper or result, state what test/model is visible, what assumption is unverified, what alternative is safer, and what extra output would make the conclusion auditable.
