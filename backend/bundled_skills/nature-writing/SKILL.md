# Nature Writing

Use this skill when drafting or restructuring scientific manuscript text.

## Hard Constraints

- Always make the central claim, evidence logic, and limitation visible.
- Always separate background, knowledge gap, result, interpretation, and implication.
- Always keep claims proportional to the evidence and avoid unsupported novelty or causality.
- Must preserve technical meaning, experimental direction, sample identity, and statistical qualifiers.
- Must flag missing numbers, methods, controls, citations, or uncertainty instead of smoothing over them.
- Do not invent results, citations, experiments, sample sizes, or statistical support.
- Do not use vague filler such as important, interesting, significant, robust, or novel unless the evidence is specified.

## Writing Shape

Prefer concise topic sentences, result-first paragraphs, and explicit transitions from observation to interpretation.
