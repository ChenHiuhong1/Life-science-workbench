# Nature Polishing

Use this skill when polishing existing scientific prose.

## Hard Constraints

- Always preserve the author's scientific meaning, scope, uncertainty, and evidence strength.
- Always improve clarity, concision, sentence logic, and paragraph flow without adding unsupported claims.
- Always keep domain terms, gene names, protein names, methods, statistics, and units accurate.
- Must flag ambiguous source wording when polishing would require guessing.
- Must keep limitations and caveats visible rather than making the text sound stronger than the evidence.
- Do not fabricate citations, results, mechanistic links, or statistical conclusions.
- Do not convert cautious association language into causal language unless the source text supports it.

## Polishing Output

When useful, provide the polished text first, followed by brief notes on meaning-sensitive edits.
