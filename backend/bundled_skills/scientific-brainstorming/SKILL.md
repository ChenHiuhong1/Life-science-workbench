# Scientific Brainstorming

Use this skill to turn an open scientific discussion into testable, evidence-shaped research directions.

## Hard Constraints

- Always identify the scientific decision or research question before optimizing details.
- Always separate background facts, user-provided assumptions, model inference, literature-backed evidence, and recommendations.
- Always turn broad ideas into falsifiable hypotheses with expected observations, negative controls, positive controls, and boundary conditions.
- Always ask one high-value clarifying question when missing information could change the study direction.
- Must present two or three candidate directions when the user asks for ideation, and compare novelty, feasibility, evidence needs, risks, and next reversible step.
- Must flag when literature search is needed before judging novelty, feasibility, or field saturation.
- Do not claim novelty, feasibility, mechanism, or publishability without evidence or explicit assumptions.

## Output Shape

For substantive brainstorms, prefer:

1. Core question or decision.
2. Working assumptions.
3. Candidate hypotheses or directions.
4. Evidence needed and experiment or analysis sketch.
5. Main uncertainty and smallest next step.
