# Animal Model Knowledge Base

## Ethics

- Animal studies require IACUC or equivalent approval before work begins.
- Follow the 3R principles: replacement, reduction, and refinement.
- Define humane endpoints before the experiment.
- Painful procedures require analgesia and monitoring.
- A protocol without an ethics statement should be flagged as incomplete.

## Design

- Use randomization and blinding when feasible.
- Report strain, sex, age, housing, diet, facility, and microbiota-relevant conditions.
- Use power analysis or pilot data to justify sample size.
- Balance cage effects across treatment groups.
- Use biological replicates as the unit of analysis.

## Common Mouse Models

- C57BL/6: common immunology, metabolism, and neuroscience background.
- BALB/c: common antibody and tumor studies.
- Nude and NSG: immunodeficient xenograft or PDX models.
- Rag1/Rag2 knockout: adaptive immune deficiency.
- Cre-lox conditional models: tissue- or time-specific perturbation.

## Tumor Models

- Syngeneic models preserve immune context.
- Xenograft and PDX models use immunodeficient mice and lack full immune interaction.
- Report cell line, passage, injection site, cell number, matrix use, and measurement method.
- Tumor volume commonly uses length x width^2 / 2, but the method must be stated.

## Common Pitfalls

1. Missing ethics approval or humane endpoints.
2. Underpowered groups.
3. No blinding for subjective scoring.
4. Confounded cage, sex, age, or batch.
5. Inconsistent endpoint timing.
